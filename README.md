## The Problem

Billions of euros are donated to NGOs and relief organizations every year — yet for donors, the central question often goes unanswered:  
**Did my money actually reach those who needed it?**

Trustfundr makes the answer public, cryptographically secure, and permanently immutable.

---

## 1. The Trust Crisis in Charitable Giving

According to multiple surveys across Europe, fewer than 40% of the population trusts that donated money is fully and purposefully used.

### Core problems:

- Annual reports and charity seals are retrospective and incomplete  
- External audits are expensive and only snapshots  
- Bank transfers disappear into a black box  
- Receipts and documents can be forged  

In crisis regions like Ukraine, the risk of misuse increases — precisely when trust is most critical.

> "Trust in NGOs is not lost — it is waiting for credible proof."

---

## 2. What Trustfundr Is

Trustfundr is a **multi-tenant, high-security donation verification and financial transparency platform**.

### Core idea

Every cent of a donation is tracked:

- From bank account entry  
- To verified delivery of aid  
- Stored in a cryptographically secured audit chain  
- Publicly accessible  

Trustfundr is **not internal accounting software** — it is public trust infrastructure.

---

## 2.1 The Lighthouse Principle

Trustfundr aims to become the **default standard**, not just an option.

This creates two effects:

- Donors demand Trustfundr audit links  
- Institutions require it for funding  

---

## 2.2 The Public Organization Profile

Each organization gets a public page: trustfundr.org/org/{slug}


### Displays in real time:

- Verified transactions (income & expenses)  
- Verified receipts (anonymized)  
- Transparency Score (0–100)  
- Delivery proofs  
- Direct donation link  

This is a **public proof of integrity**, not just a dashboard.

---

## 3. The Audit Chain

Three mandatory stages:

| Stage | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Stage 1 — Income | All accounts connected via banking API |
| Stage 2 — Expenditure | OCR-verified receipts required |
| Stage 3 — Delivery | Proof via photo, video, or platform |

### Security mechanisms:

- Error Level Analysis (ELA)  
- Exif metadata analysis  
- OCR cross-validation  
- Immutable audit chain  
- Face recognition & OSINT removal  

---

## 4. Transparency Score

Each organization gets a **score (0–100)**.

| Factor | Behavior |
|--------|--------|
| Audit completeness | Core factor |
| Nonprofit status | Bonus |
| Submission speed | Positive impact |
| Anomalies | Reset to 0 |
| Technical delays | 7-day grace |
| Inactivity | Score decay after 4 weeks |

This is **not a seal**, but a continuous evaluation.

---

## 5. Security Architecture

Designed for adversarial threats (including state-level actors).

### 5.1 Zero-Trust

- No implicit trust  
- mTLS for all communication  
- Secrets via HashiCorp Vault  
- MFA required (no email OTPs)  

### 5.2 Data Sovereignty

- Hosting in Switzerland & Ukraine  
- No AWS, Google Cloud, Azure  

### 5.3 Authority Access

- Dedicated auditor module (Module 22)  
- Access to original documents  
- Fully logged

## 10. Trustfundr as a Nonprofit Organization

Trustfundr itself is designed to be established as a **registered nonprofit organization in Ukraine**.

This has three structural implications:

- Trustfundr will be subject to **full regulatory oversight by Ukrainian authorities**  
- All financial activities of Trustfundr itself will be **transparent and auditable**  
- The platform operates under the same transparency principles it enforces  

This ensures that Trustfundr is not only a transparency provider, but also **fully accountable within a regulated legal framework**.
